Signer Denial Risks

R-01

Signer Denial Claims

"I never signed that." — Standard eSignatures lack cryptographic intent binding, making it difficult to prove authorship in court. Without a direct link between the user's private key and the specific document version, the burden of proof remains on the business.

R-02

Timestamp Disputes

Unclear approval timing due to reliance on server-side clocks that can be out of sync or manipulated. BrillSign solves this by using decentralized, immutable timestamps that provide an indisputable record of exactly when an action occurred.

R-03

Platform Evidence Dependency

Evidence relies on proprietary vendor logs; without their active testimony and platform availability, your signatures may be invalid. Traditional vendors create a "walled garden" where you lose access to your own proof if you switch providers.

R-04

Log Tampering Risk

Centralized logs are vulnerable to unauthorized edits by administrators or malicious actors, compromising the integrity of the audit trail. Once a log is modified, the entire chain of trust is broken, rendering the eSignature factually useless.

R-05

Identity Spoofing

Standard email-based authentication is prone to session hijacking and spoofed identities, failing to provide strong non-repudiation. High-value transactions require multi-factor, biometric-backed proof that cannot be easily replicated or stolen.
