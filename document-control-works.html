The Sovereignty Split

Understanding the fundamental difference between centralized hosting and decentralized document control.

Centralized Storage

High-risk dependency on vendor continuity.

  • Vendor owns the storage keys
  • Access depends on platform uptime
  • Documents are trapped if vendor folds
  • Single point of security failure

BrillSign Control

Total autonomy and distributed integrity.

  • You own the sovereign access keys
  • Documents accessible via IPFS nodes
  • Independent of BrillSign platform
  • Zero-Knowledge redundant network

Adaptive Redundancy

BrillSign uses a distributed topology to ensure your agreements never go offline.

Sharded Distribution

Documents are encrypted and sharded across a global network of independent nodes.

Automatic Healing

Our network automatically detects and replaces inactive nodes to maintain document availability.

End-to-End Sovereignty

Even BrillSign nodes cannot read your content; they only host the encrypted shards you control.
