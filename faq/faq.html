No. BrillSign uses a zero-knowledge architecture, meaning documents are encrypted on your device before they ever reach our servers. We never hold your encryption keys, so it is architecturally impossible for any BrillSign employee — or our systems — to access your document contents.
BrillSign uses AES-256 client-side encryption for document contents, combined with elliptic curve cryptography (ECC) for digital signatures. Encryption keys are derived from your organization's identity and are never transmitted to or stored on BrillSign servers.
Instead of exposing document contents, BrillSign generates a cryptographic fingerprint (hash) of your document. This fingerprint is used to verify authenticity and integrity without ever revealing the actual data. The verification can be performed by anyone, independently, without needing access to BrillSign's systems.
Even in the unlikely event of a breach, attackers would only find encrypted ciphertext — with no keys stored on our servers. Your documents remain completely unreadable. Additionally, encrypted shards are distributed across multiple nodes, so there's no single point of exposure.
