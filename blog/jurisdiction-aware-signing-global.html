A multinational company signs contracts across 30 countries. Each country has different rules about what makes a digital signature legally binding. Different authentication requirements. Different data residency mandates. Different timestamp and witness regulations.

Traditionally, this means either hiring specialized legal counsel for every cross-border deal, or accepting the risk that some signed documents might not hold up in a foreign court. Both options are expensive. BrillSign offers a third way.

The borderless problem

Digital business has no borders, but digital law does. When a US-based SaaS company signs a data processing agreement with a German enterprise, the contract must comply with both US and EU standards. eIDAS in Europe mandates different signature levels — simple, advanced, and qualified — each with increasing authentication requirements.

Most e-signature platforms treat all signatures the same regardless of geography. This creates a hidden compliance gap: your contract might be signed, but is it legally defensible in every jurisdiction it touches?

"We used to escalate every cross-border deal to our legal team just to verify signature requirements. Now BrillSign handles jurisdiction detection automatically — our close rate improved by 40%."

How BrillSign's jurisdiction detection works

Navigating the global regulatory landscape

The world's major digital signature frameworks — eIDAS (EU), ESIGN Act (USA), IT Act (India), ECA (Canada), and others — share common principles but differ in critical details. BrillSign maintains an updated compliance matrix covering 150+ jurisdictions.

  • European Union (eIDAS): Requires qualified electronic signatures for highest legal weight. BrillSign automatically elevates to QES when EU signers are detected
  • United States (ESIGN/UETA): Broad acceptance of electronic signatures. BrillSign adds cryptographic non-repudiation for maximum defensibility
  • India (IT Act 2000): Requires Aadhaar or DSC-based verification for certain document types. BrillSign routes through compliant identity providers
  • Middle East (Various): Some jurisdictions require government-approved digital certificates. BrillSign integrates with local PKI infrastructure

Real-world use cases

BrillSign handles this by creating jurisdiction-specific signing workflows that adapt automatically: higher authentication in Germany, specific stamp requirements in Japan, bilingual witness requirements in Canada's Quebec province.
