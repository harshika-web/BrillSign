Here's a question most enterprises never ask their e-signature vendor: "If we stop paying you, or if you shut down, will our signed documents still be legally valid?"

For most platforms, the answer is uncomfortable. Your documents are stored on their servers, verified through their proprietary systems, and their legal validity depends on their continued operation. When the vendor goes away, so does your proof.

BrillSign was built from day one to make sure that never happens.

The vendor lock-in trap

Traditional e-signature platforms create a dependency that most organizations don't recognize until it's too late. Your documents live on their infrastructure. Verification requires their code. Audit trails route through their servers. You're not just renting a tool — you're outsourcing the legal validity of your contracts.

This creates three distinct risks:

  • Vendor failure: If the company shuts down, goes bankrupt, or is acquired and deprecated, your document verification chain breaks
  • Platform migration: Switching vendors means potentially re-signing thousands of documents or maintaining perpetual legacy access
  • Price leverage: When your vendor controls the validity of your contracts, they have extraordinary leverage over renewal pricing

What exit-safe really means

Exit-safe architecture means that every signature BrillSign creates is independently verifiable without BrillSign's involvement. We embed all cryptographic proof directly into the signed document itself. No phone-home. No API call. No dependency on our servers.

"We evaluated BrillSign specifically because they were the only vendor willing to architect themselves out of the verification chain. That told us everything about their long-term intentions."

A migration story

A mid-market law firm with 15,000 signed contracts decided to switch from their legacy e-signature vendor. The migration estimate? 18 months and $200,000 in re-processing costs. Many documents required re-signing because the verification chain was proprietary.

After switching to BrillSign, they signed 20,000 new documents over two years. When they later needed to export everything for an acquisition due diligence, the entire archive — with full verification proofs — was exported in under an hour. Every document remained independently verifiable. Total migration cost for the next vendor switch: zero.

Future-proofing your contracts

Contracts can have legal relevance for decades. Employment agreements, IP assignments, real estate deeds — these documents need to be verifiable 10, 20, or 50 years from now. No SaaS vendor can credibly guarantee they'll exist that long.

BrillSign's approach acknowledges this reality. Instead of asking you to trust us forever, we build proof directly into every document. Your legal foundation doesn't depend on our business model. That's exit-safe architecture.
