The Challenge

Paper consent was slowing patient care

As one of the largest hospital networks on the East Coast, the organization handles thousands of patient consent forms daily — from surgical authorizations and treatment plans to clinical trial enrollments and insurance pre-approvals.

Their paper-based consent system was creating dangerous inefficiencies:

25-Minute Consent Process

Patients waited an average of 25 minutes for consent forms to be printed, signed, witnessed, scanned, and filed — delaying procedures and frustrating staff.

Lost & Misfiled Consents

3.2% of paper consent forms were misfiled or lost annually, creating legal liability and requiring patients to re-sign — sometimes after procedures had already begun.

HIPAA Audit Exposure

The paper trail was nearly impossible to audit. The compliance team spent 6 weeks annually preparing for HIPAA audits with no guarantee of completeness.

"We had a case where a surgical consent form was lost between floors. The surgeon couldn't proceed, the patient was already prepped, and we had to re-do the entire consent process in the OR hallway. That was the breaking point."

Dr. Sarah Lin

The Solution

BrillSign's zero-knowledge platform for healthcare compliance

After a 60-day pilot in the surgical department — including a detailed HIPAA security assessment and integration testing with the network's Epic EMR system — all 15 hospitals migrated to BrillSign.

HIPAA-Native Architecture

BrillSign's zero-knowledge design means patient health information (PHI) is never accessible to BrillSign — exceeding HIPAA's "minimum necessary" standard by eliminating third-party access entirely.

Bedside Digital Consent

Patients sign consent forms on bedside tablets or their own phones. Forms auto-populate from the EMR, reducing errors and eliminating paper entirely. Average consent time: 5 minutes.

EMR Auto-Sync

Signed consent documents are automatically linked back to the patient's electronic medical record via BrillSign's API — no manual scanning, filing, or data entry required.

Immutable Consent Records

Every consent event generates a cryptographically sealed audit trail. HIPAA auditors can verify the entire consent chain in minutes — replacing 6 weeks of manual preparation.

Implementation

Phased rollout across 15 hospitals

The rollout was designed to start with the highest-volume department, prove the workflow, then expand rapidly across the entire network with minimal disruption to patient care.

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3

Surgical Department Pilot

Deployed at the flagship hospital's surgical department — 40 surgeons, 800 consent forms/week. Validated EMR integration, bedside tablet workflow, and audit trail export with the HIPAA compliance team.

Phase 2 — Weeks 4–6

Emergency & Outpatient Expansion

Extended to the emergency department and outpatient clinics across 5 hospitals. Added mobile phone consent for patients who preferred their own devices. Integrated with the insurance pre-approval system.

Phase 3 — Weeks 7–10

Full Network Rollout

Migrated all 15 hospitals including pediatrics, oncology, and clinical trials. Trained 4,200+ staff members. Decommissioned paper consent and shredded archived physical forms after digital verification.

Ongoing

Continuous Compliance

HIPAA audit preparation now takes 2 hours instead of 6 weeks. The network is exploring BrillSign's API for automated clinical trial consent and multi-language patient forms.
