The Challenge

Legacy signing infrastructure couldn't keep up

As one of the top 10 banks by assets in North America, the organization manages hundreds of thousands of agreements annually — from retail mortgage applications to institutional lending contracts and cross-border wealth management documents.

Their existing e-signature provider was creating systemic problems that went far beyond inconvenience:

14-Day Contract Cycles

Multi-party agreements required manual compliance checks at every stage, creating weeks of delay for even routine contracts.

Cross-Border Friction

European wealth management clients required eIDAS-compliant signatures, but the legacy system couldn't differentiate between jurisdictions.

Vendor Data Access

The existing provider's architecture meant sensitive financial documents were accessible to vendor employees — a major regulatory concern.

"We were spending more time proving our contracts were valid than negotiating them. When regulators asked about our vendor's access to documents, we didn't have a satisfactory answer. That's when we knew we needed a fundamentally different architecture."
JM
James Mercer
SVP, Legal Operations
The Solution

BrillSign's zero-knowledge platform for regulated finance

After a 90-day evaluation that included security architecture review, regulatory impact assessment, and a pilot program with the retail lending division, the bank migrated to BrillSign across all business lines.

Zero-Knowledge Architecture

BrillSign is architecturally incapable of accessing document contents. This resolved the bank's most critical regulatory concern — third-party vendor access to sensitive financial data.

Jurisdiction-Aware Signing

The platform automatically detects signer jurisdictions and applies the correct legal standard — ESIGN for US domestic, eIDAS for European clients, and qualified certificates where required.

Immutable Audit Trails

Every signature event generates a cryptographically sealed record. Compliance teams export a complete Regulatory Disclosure Package in one click — eliminating weeks of audit preparation.

Exit-Safe Architecture

All signed documents contain self-verifying cryptographic proofs. Even if the bank migrates vendors in future, every document remains independently verifiable — forever.

Implementation

Phased rollout across 3 business lines

The implementation was designed to minimize disruption while maximizing early proof-of-value. The bank's IT team worked with BrillSign's enterprise deployment specialists to execute a three-phase migration.

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4

Retail Lending Pilot

Deployed BrillSign for the retail mortgage division — 200 loan officers, 3,000 agreements/month. Validated jurisdiction detection, signing workflows, and audit trail export with the compliance team.

Phase 2 — Weeks 5–8

Institutional & Wealth Management

Expanded to institutional lending and cross-border wealth management. Activated eIDAS qualified signatures for European clients and integrated with the bank's existing DMS via BrillSign's API.

Phase 3 — Weeks 9–12

Full Enterprise Rollout

Migrated remaining divisions including HR, procurement, and vendor agreements. Decommissioned legacy provider. Conducted organization-wide training for 40,000+ employees across 12 offices.

Ongoing

Continuous Optimization

Monthly compliance reviews now take 2 hours instead of 2 weeks. The bank's innovation team is exploring BrillSign's API for automated NDA generation and AI-assisted contract review.
